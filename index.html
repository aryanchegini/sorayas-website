Our lives are unfolding in a context of increasing unease and uncertainty. The demands and perpetual hum of our busy lives often creates fog and overwhelm. Unexpected life events paralyse us.

We can feel lost and disconnected from ourselves, our lives and relationships; stuck in endless cycles and unhealthy patterns. It sometimes feels all paths lead to nowhere.

I offer a space to rest, take a breath and take the weight off. To sit back and gently untangle what is in front of us.

Connecting back to ourselves is essential in uncovering what truly needs tending to. From here, light and potential can be found. We can open up to fresh possibility and a new direction.

My Approach

As a transpersonal integrative psychotherapist. I am clinically trained in multiple modalities. This allows me to adapt my approach to meet your unique needs.

Transpersonal means that my approach is holistic; bringing the body, mind and soul in harmony to accomplish healing and growth. If sometimes language evades us, we can work with creative expression, dreams and mindfulness to create breakthroughs. This can mean listening to the body or checking in with the senses whilst we work, connecting with what is true and authentic.

I believe therapy is a collaborative process of careful listening and supportive dialogue. By creating a secure foundation of trust and safety, we allow unconscious patterns and self-limiting beliefs to move into the light to be witnessed and released. Most importantly, our work together will help cultivate an inner guidance for you to take out into your life and relationships.

Whatever the issue, I aim to walk alongside you to uncover your concerns, untangle what is stuck and be there to witness and celebrate your progress.

The work I do with clients includes:

  • Childhood Trauma
  • Domestic Abuse
  • PTSD and Complex PTSD
  • Race, identity and cultural issues
  • Co-dependency and relationship issues
  • Depression
  • Low self-esteem
  • Anxiety and social anxiety
  • Bereavement and loss
  • Corporate disillusionment and burnout
  • Midlife transition/ depression
  • Spiritual Crisis

About Me

After studying Psychology at Leeds University, I spent 20+ years in the fashion retail industry, leading large teams in creative and project management. The corporate world delivered not only success and accomplishment but also chronic exhaustion and disillusionment.

My path had brought personal struggle and inner turmoil. I’d sought answers in the wrong places. I embarked on a new journey of discovery through a blend of therapy, shamanic work, meditation, Buddhist and tantra practice. These have all supported me to turn inward and heal, becoming sacred practices to integrate into my work as a therapist.

My formal training is in integrative and transpersonal psychotherapy; receiving a Post-Graduate diploma at the Centre of Counselling and Psychotherapy Education (CCPE) and I'm also a member of the UKCP.

I have trauma training experience in supporting those who have suffered emotional, physical or sexual abuse in childhood.

FAQs

Where are your practice locations?

I am based in Margate (CT9) and also practice in Paddington, London (W2). Additionally, I offer online sessions.



What is included in the introductory session?

I offer a complimentary 30-minute introductory session. This serves as an opportunity for you to gauge if we are a good fit. There's no obligation to continue therapy beyond this session.



How long is a typical therapy session, and how often should sessions occur?

Therapy sessions typically last 50 minutes and are ideally scheduled once a week.



How is the progress of therapy reviewed?

We will regularly review the progress of therapy sessions together. It's entirely up to you to decide the number of sessions you feel you need. Therapy can last anywhere from a few weeks to several years based on individual requirements.
