We live against a backdrop of unease and the perpetual hum of demand on our time and energy can bring a fog of overwhelm.

We just can’t get a moment…

We can feel lost and disconnected from ourselves, stuck in perpetual cycles. Our lives and relationships feel confused. It sometimes feels all paths lead to nowhere.

I offer a space to rest, take the weight off, take a breath, take the weight off,. To sit back and gently untangle what is in front of us.

Connecting back to ourselves is essential in order to notice what we need to attend to. From here light and potential can be found, so we open up to fresh possibility and a new direction.

My Approach

A transpersonal integrative psychotherapist, I am clinically trained in multiple modalities. This allows me to adjust to your unique needs.

I aim to walk alongside you throughout your progress as we gently uncover what concerns you.
Therapy is a collaborative process of careful listening and supportive dialogue. If sometimes language evades us, we can work with creative expression, dreams and mindfulness. My approach is holistic; bringing the body, mind and soul in harmony to accomplish healing and growth. This can mean listening to the body or checking in with the senses whilst we work, connecting with what is true and authentic.

The therapeutic relationship is the core of my work. It takes time to build trust and safety. Through this process a secure foundation can grow. Unconscious patterns and self-limiting beliefs move into the light to be witnessed and released. This creates the confidence to tread unexplored paths. This work will help cultivate an inner guidance to take out into your life and relationships.

Some of the issues I have helped clients with:

  • Anxiety and social anxiety
  • Bereavement and loss
  • Co-dependency and relationship issues
  • Depression
  • Low self-esteem
  • Narcissism and narcissistic abuse
  • Post-traumatic stress
  • Corporate disillusionment and burnout
  • Midlife transition

About Me

After studying Psychology at Leeds University I spent 23 years in the fashion retail industry leading large teams in creative and project management. The corporate world brought not only success and accomplishment but also delivered chronic exhaustion and disillusionment.
I was left wondering ‘Is this it?’

My journey left me grappling with personal struggle and inner turmoil. I’d sought answers in the wrong places. I embarked on a new journey of discovery through a blend of therapy, shamanic work, Buddhist and tantra practice and meditation. These have all supported me to turn inward and heal, allowing valuable and sacred practices to integrate into my work as a therapist.

My formal training is in integrative and transpersonal Psychotherapy. I also have trauma training experience in supporting those who have suffered emotional, physical or sexual abuse in childhood.

FAQs

Where are your practice locations?

I am based in Margate and also practice in Paddington, London (W2), and Faversham, Kent throughout the week. Additionally, I offer online sessions.



What is included in the introductory session?

I offer a complimentary 20-minute introductory session. This serves as an opportunity for you to gauge if we are a good fit. There's no obligation to continue therapy beyond this session.



How long is a typical therapy session, and how often should sessions occur?

Therapy sessions typically last 50 minutes and are ideally scheduled once a week.



How is the progress of therapy reviewed?

We will regularly review the progress of therapy sessions together. It's entirely up to you to decide the number of sessions you feel you need. Therapy can last anywhere from a few weeks to several years based on individual requirements.
